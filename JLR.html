Abstract / Overview

Research Overview

This research explores the impact of Trustpilot reviews and social media feedback on consumer behaviour toward Jaguar Land Rover (JLR), a British premium automotive manufacturer headquartered in Whitley, Coventry. Using a qualitative mono-method approach, the study analyses archival data scraped from Trustpilot (jlr.com and landrover.com) and employs NVivo for thematic analysis and Voyant Tools for word frequency visualisation. Sentiment analysis of 1,543 reviews reveals that 72% carry negative sentiment — primarily driven by poor customer service, unresolved electrical faults, and warranty failures. Positive reviews (19%) highlight premium design, performance, and occasional exceptional dealer experiences. The research proposes the EFQM model as a strategic tool for aligning JLR's operations with customer expectations and recommends predictive analytics, pricing transparency, and customer-centric training programmes.

Jaguar Land Rover Automotive PLC is the holding company of JLR Limited — a British multinational automotive company headquartered in Whitley, Coventry, and a subsidiary of Tata Motors (India). JLR manufactures premium vehicles including the Range Rover, Discovery, Defender, and Jaguar model lines. Despite the brand's luxury positioning, customer feedback data from Trustpilot reveals a significant and persistent gap between brand promise and customer experience delivery.

⚠️
Research Problem: JLR holds a Trustpilot rating of just 1.6/5 across 1,543+ reviews — placing it in the "Bad" category. The dominant complaint theme is poor customer service (identified in over 60% of negative reviews), followed by unresolved electrical faults, warranty refusals, and communication failures. This research applies text analytics to extract structured insights from this unstructured feedback and propose evidence-based market strategy improvements.
Chapter 3 — Methodology

Research Design & Methodology

The Research Onion framework (Saunders et al., 2019) guides the methodological choices, from philosophical stance through to data collection techniques.

1

Research Philosophy

Interpretivist paradigm — capturing the subjective experiences and sentiments expressed by JLR customers in unstructured Trustpilot reviews.

2

Research Approach

Inductive approach — themes and patterns derived bottom-up from customer review data, not imposed from pre-existing hypotheses.

3

Methodological Choice

Qualitative mono-method — focus on rich textual data from Trustpilot to capture depth of customer sentiment and complaint patterns.

4

Research Strategy

Archival research — scraping existing Trustpilot reviews using Octoparse. No primary interviews needed; secondary data reflects genuine, unsolicited customer voice.

5

Time Horizon

Cross-sectional — snapshot of JLR customer reviews published between 2023 and 2025, capturing current sentiment at a specific period.

6

Data Analysis

NVivo for thematic coding of reviews. Voyant Tools for word frequency visualisation, word cloud, trend analysis, and word link network mapping.

Chapter 5.2 — Conclusion

Recommendations

Based on the text analytics findings from Trustpilot review analysis, the following six strategic recommendations are proposed for Jaguar Land Rover to close the gap between brand promise and customer experience delivery:

  • 1

    Customer Service Personnel Training

    JLR should invest in structured training programmes for both dealer network staff and JLR direct contact centres. Programmes should focus on empathetic communication, complaint escalation protocols, and resolution ownership. "Poor Customer Service" represents 687 NVivo-coded references — the single largest driver of negative reviews and the most immediately addressable.

  • 2

    Transparent Warranty & Fault Resolution Process

    JLR should publish clear warranty escalation pathways and guarantee response SLAs. Many negative reviews describe customers being unable to contact senior management or get warranty claims acknowledged. A structured 24–72 hour acknowledgement process, modelled on BMW's Client Relations framework, would directly address 319 coded warranty failure references.

  • 3

    Predictive Analytics for Recurring Electrical Faults

    JLR should implement a predictive analytics system that cross-references service record data with Trustpilot / social media complaint trends. The 412 references to unresolved electrical faults (battery, I-Pace charging, F-Pace power steering) indicate systemic issues known to JLR engineers but not proactively communicated to affected customers. Early text mining detection could trigger proactive recall notices before social amplification.

  • 4

    Structured Customer Feedback Culture

    JLR should deploy active sentiment monitoring — using tools like Brandwatch or Sprinklr — to track Trustpilot, Reddit, and automotive forum sentiment in real time. Reviews should be fed into a monthly Voice of Customer dashboard presented at board level, mirroring the approach used by Tata Consultancy Services for enterprise feedback loops.

  • 5

    Communication Response SLAs

    With 271 references to communication failures — customers unable to reach senior executives, emails unanswered, phone lines dead — JLR must establish mandatory response SLAs: 4 hours for complaint acknowledgement, 48 hours for case assignment, 14 days for formal resolution. Public SLA commitments (published on the JLR website) create accountability and reduce escalation to Trustpilot reviews.

  • 6

    EFQM-Aligned Customer Results Integration

    JLR should formally adopt the EFQM Excellence Model as its quality management framework, with "Customer Results" as the primary performance criterion. Quarterly EFQM self-assessments, scored against Trustpilot sentiment, NPS, and warranty resolution data, would create the continuous improvement loop needed to shift from a 72% negative sentiment score to the premium brand benchmark of <30% negative feedback within 18 months.
